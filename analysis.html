STRR impact on each area

Satellite Town Ring Road (STRR Phase 1 ~2027) is the single biggest infrastructure catalyst for most of these areas.

  • Yelahanka NT: becomes an STRR interchange suburb — biggest orbital boost
  • Yelahanka SH-9: SH-9/Doddaballapura alignment is on STRR route — direct benefit to Sattva Lumina
  • Hosahalli: outer belt alignment passes through — swing catalyst for Airport Road
  • Devanahalli: essential prerequisite for self-use viability — cuts Manyata run to ~40–50 min
  • Thanisandra: limited direct STRR benefit; ORR is the relevant orbital; Metro Phase 2B is bigger catalyst

Loading factor — the trap to avoid

Builders advertise Super Built-up Area (SBA). What you live in is Carpet Area. Always ask for the RERA-registered carpet area certificate. Post-2017, RERA mandates disclosure.

Builder (est. loading)At 1,700 sqft SBACarpet areaVerdict
Tata Housing (~24%)1,700 sqft SBA~1,292 sqft carpetBest on list
Puravankara (~27%)1,700 sqft SBA~1,241 sqft carpetGood
Prestige (~29%)1,700 sqft SBA~1,207 sqft carpetAcceptable
Sattva/Brigade (~31%)1,700 sqft SBA~1,173 sqft carpetVerify carefully
Poor project (~35%)1,700 sqft SBA~1,105 sqft carpetReject

Rule: Loading above 33% → walk away. Always convert price to ₹/sqft carpet, not SBA. A builder who hesitates to share RERA carpet area is a red flag.

Why Grade A only for UC
  • NSE/BSE-listed builders must disclose finances quarterly — no silent fund diversions
  • RERA escrow: 70% of buyer money must sit in project-specific account
  • Institutional lenders (HDFC, SBI) already approved → buyers get easier loans at resale
  • Brand value creates a price floor — even in distressed market, Grade A holds better
  • Track record: all five builders have delivered projects in North BLR before
Watch-outs per builder
  • Puravankara: After-sales inconsistent across projects; verify specific society
  • Prestige: Some projects ran 6–12 months late post-COVID; verify RERA % now
  • Sattva: Older projects had 6–9 month delays; check tower-wise RERA dates
  • Brigade: Possession date discrepancy (Mar vs Dec 2030) — resolve in writing
  • Tata: Very new launch (Aug 2025) — verify construction physically started on site

Legal & Documentation

Criterion 5 · red flags · due diligence process · document checklist

🚨 Red flags — walk away immediately

Red flagSeverityWhy it matters
No OC (Occupancy Certificate)Walk awayIllegal construction. No bank loan possible. No legal water/electricity. Demolition risk.
B-Khata propertyWalk awayBuilt on illegally converted land. Resale near-impossible. BBMP can penalise or demolish.
Encumbrance not cleanWalk awayExisting mortgage, court dispute, or lien. You inherit the problem on purchase.
No DC Conversion order (ex-agricultural land)Walk awayResidential construction technically invalid. Any approval was itself illegal.
Construction deviates from sanctioned planHigh riskDeviated floors/area can be demolished. Common in older Bangalore buildings.
Seller unwilling to produce documentsWalk awayAny hesitation = something hidden. A clean property has all documents ready.
Property tax dues pendingNegotiateDues transfer to you post-registration. Clear dues before or deduct from price.
Undisclosed co-ownersHigh riskAll co-owners must sign the sale deed. Missing signature = invalid transaction.
Price significantly below marketCautionDistress sales can be legitimate but often hide legal issues. Extra due diligence required.

Document checklist — request before signing anything

  • Occupancy Certificate (OC) — builder / BBMP issued Critical
  • A-Khata certificate from BBMP — not B-Khata Critical
  • Encumbrance Certificate (Form 15/16) — minimum 15 years Critical
  • Title deed chain — original owner to current seller
  • BBMP/BDA sanctioned building plan — matches actual construction
  • Property tax receipts — last 5 years, no dues
  • DC Conversion order — if built on ex-agricultural land
  • Betterment charges paid receipt (BBMP)
  • Society NOC — all dues cleared by seller
  • Bank NOC + original title deeds if seller has/had a loan
  • Previous sale deeds — full chain of ownership
  • RERA carpet area disclosure certificate
  • Completion Certificate (CC) — from BBMP/BDA
  • BWSSB water connection approval / NOC
  • BESCOM electricity connection in building name
